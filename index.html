About Me

I'm a graduate student at Yokohama City University (YCU) with a unique journey bridging industry experience and academic research. With over 5 years of professional experience as a full-stack web engineer, I've developed expertise in both frontend and backend development across various technologies and frameworks.


My path took an interesting turn when I decided to pursue formal computer science education to strengthen my foundational knowledge in mathematics, statistics, and theoretical computer science. Coming from an Economics background, I recognized the importance of building solid technical fundamentals to advance my career in technology.


Currently, I'm actively researching in a Machine Learning lab, with a particular focus on Virtual Try-On applications. My research interests center around cutting-edge techniques including Diffusion Models and Transformers, exploring how these powerful architectures can revolutionize the fashion and e-commerce industries through realistic virtual garment fitting experiences.
